Stackademy summarized

Berlin's tech scene is growing fast and attracts talent from all over the world.
With Stackademy, we want to add the first-rate programming course to the mix that we ourselves would want to participate in and are seeking an experienced programmer-instructor to join us on our mission: offering a life-changing, intense learning experience in Europe's central capital Berlin.

Taking a cue from successful programs in the U.S., we aim to set up Stackademy in similar fashion:

  • 14-20 participants, selected through an application process
  • a mandatory preparatory phase in advance for all participants
  • 30% - 70% division of lessons vs. practical exercises
  • a full stack approach to web application development:
    • backend development using Ruby and the Ruby on Rails framework
    • database basics
    • HTML/CSS and frameworks
    • JavaScript basics
  • course work in pair programming approach
  • use of 3rd party APIs in course projects
  • inclusion of tools and methods for effective work (version control, editor setup, preprocessors, ...)

To help participants with smaller problems and issues during the practical exercises, we will bring onboard teaching assistants from Berlin.

We offer a salary of EUR 30,000 - 35,000 for four months of preparatory work and teaching, and are open to adjust for workload and your own background. We will of course assist with the administrative work related to your stay.

Let us know if Stackademy has made you curious: You can reach Jonas at / @jdker.

About us

Both of us studied business and got involved with startups in different ways. We also spent time in Berlin and over time realized we wanted to grow beyond just using software and start building it.

With the many great, free online courses, it would seem learning to code has gotten very easy. When we started with these, though, we noted that do achieve the great jump ahead that we sought, an online course was not the way to go. Instead, we believe an intense full-time course has the highest potential for exceptional results when approached the right way, and decided to establish such a course in Berlin.

Partner companies

We are working with three kinds of partners:

  • Recruiting partners: Offering exciting employment opportunities to participants complements the course in an ideal way.
    We are in touch with companies that provide the right environment and are looking for new tech talent.
  • Startups: Berlin startups are starting to become more serious and professional in their approach, and we want to hear from those that are working on great products. We already have confirmations from Berlin startups for guest lectures, e.g. on the way that major social APIs are integrated into a product.
  • Technology partners: Tech projects in the real world make clever use of third-party services and we want participants to gain experience in working with the leading providers in the respective space, from payment to deployment.
    Apart from trial use of the product during the course, we are looking to get companies to contribute guest lectures on the advanced aspects of their own platform.

Wooga

Wooga is one of the world's largest social games developers with 50m active monthly users across eight games

Founded in 2009 by Jens Begemann, Philipp Moeser and Patrick Paulisch, Wooga today employs more than 250 people at its Backfabrik offices in Berlin.

Games are developed and maintained by independent teams and deployed on Amazon's Web Services infrastructure, and Wooga employees are frequent contributors to user groups and meetups in their respective field.

Recruiting Partner

Paymill

Paymill provides a developer-focused payment solution for tech companies, similar to Stripe in the U.S.

Based in Munich, Germany, Paymill currently supports 14 currencies and 34 different countries, including all EU countries. As a result, most national payment services can be integrated as well as all major credit cards.

Paymill has made API kits available for PHP, Java, Python, node.js, Ruby and .NET.

Technology Partner

Moped

Started in late 2011, Moped is a free messaging application available in the browser and on mobile platforms that ties into services like Dropbox, Foursquare, IFTTT, Twitter and Facebook.

The company received seed financing from betaworks, Lerer Ventures, SV Angel and Berlin VC Earlybird and has steadily grown its platform reach and product integrations. Moped today is a team of six in its Berlin HQ, including founder Schuyler Deerman.
